#### [Honors Thesis](https://github.com/finnmeffe/honors-thesis) - Fragmentation and firm choice: The effects of municipal decentralization on firm-level agglomeration economies

<kdb>
  <img width="915" height="329" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/ca5ff607-8a1d-4746-8147-bc8d8eb7fc84" />
</kdb>

I explore the relationship between the political arrangments within U.S. metropolitan areas (often referred to as a type of "tapestry" or "mosaic" of local jurisdictions) and the arrangment of firms across space: does local competition act as a barrier to clustering of economic activity? My identification strategy uses the "small streams" approach pioneered by Hoxby (2000) as a source of exogenous variation in political borders, which I regress against a set of constructed global agglomeration measures at the metropolitan level. I find that more fragmentation causes greater firm agglomeration, with each additional municipality leading to a 0.4% increase in the probability of agglomeration at the metropolitan level.

#### [Final Project](https://github.com/finnmeffe/urban-analytics-final) - Cyclical Growth: Demographic and Geographic Drivers of Cycling Uptake

<kdb>
<img width="1296" height="593" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/6e747997-0db9-40df-8ced-1238c7921ebd" />
</kdb>

I investigate the role of different factors in determining why biking is popular in some areas and completely absent from others. I build a small ladder of models that work to complement each other. These include linear regression as a baseline, LASSO to shrinks and select from a wide demographic + climate + terrain feature matrix, spatial lag and spatial error models to handle residual autocorrelation, and a feed-forward neural network to test whether non-linear interactions buy meaningful predictive gain. I find that cultural features, such as the share of residents with a bachelor's degree and political leanings, are responsible for a significant amount of bike usage, rather than simply geographic and infrastructural amenities. I also build a Shiny app for data visualization, test biking sentiment through LLM analysis, and conduct a comparison with a policy counterfactual to show meaningful gains from employing my predictive findings.

#### [Term Paper](https://github.com/finnmeffe/political-economy-paper) - Urbanization and democracy: primate cities as democratic incubators

<kdb>

This term paper examines the role of urbanization in fostering democratic movements. I make use of the V-Dem dataset to capture democratic indices, and UN World Urbanization Prospects data for panel data on urbanization. I adopt various econometric strategies to solve persistent endogeneity/reverse causality issues: lagged regression, instrumental variables, and event study. While the majority of effects are null, I do find some evidence that urbanization can foster an environment in which democratic reform is more likely.
